  • Credit history: FirstLoan considers multiple factors when assessing a borrower’s application, including their credit history. This approach ensures that borrowers receive a fair evaluation based on their financial background.
  • FirstLoan is a short-term online installment loan firm operating from the territory of a Native American tribe and following tribal regulations. They offer various loan programs and cater to different loan types.

What is a FirstLoan?

FirstLoan is a short-term installment loan firm that operates online. It is strikingly similar to the justifications used by payday lenders. They understand that these loans are an “expensive form of borrowing.” It only is utilized in times of extreme financial need. People have to come up with the money for things like rent, unexpected expenses for car repairs, medical expenses such as bills, and emergency expenses like emergency room visits.

FirstLoan addresses similarities since they might hurt enterprises. FirstLoan argues that their loans are better since they provide flexible repayment alternatives and cash when required.

The two types of loans, specifically personal loans, are similar regarding practical use. They’re both due soon, but they share practically everything else. They’re both rather costly but accessible in a small amount of principal and are meant to help in emergencies. Loans are easily accessible to everybody, even those who struggle to make payments, providing wider access to loans.

Is FirstLoan a licensed company?

Yes, FirstLoan is a licensed company because of its location in California. Tribal loans don’t have state licenses. FirstLoan must register with the Department of Business Oversight (DBO). The DBO’s primary mission is to ensure customers’ safety and assist companies in facilitating commerce in the financial sector. A wide range of financial products, services, and experts are subject to the Department’s oversight.

They operate from a Native American tribe’s territory and follow their tribal regulations. Tribal immunity exempts them from state and federal legislation. They often offer loan rates greater than state laws. California’s maximum annual interest rate is 460%, yet FirstLoan’s rate sample is 778%.

FirstLoan makes its tribal status clear in its fine print, like many other tribal lenders. According to their website, “The Elem Indian Colony of Pomo Indians, as a sovereign Tribal nation within the United States, operates FirstLoan, a Native American-owned business. All applicable federal laws and regulations, as well as tribal law established by the Elem Indian Colony of Pomo Indians, are followed by FirstLoan.”

It implies that they tolerate government restrictions, but they refer to a federal statute that states that Native American tribes have control over their lands. State governments have complete control over payday lenders. Thus, federal legislation has no impact.

Considering FirstLoan’s offerings, some of the most commonly used loan phrases involve monthly payments and the remaining loan balance. These terms help borrowers understand the amount they must pay each month and the outstanding debt they still need to settle. By being aware of these keywords, individuals can better manage their loans and navigate the financial landscape offered by FirstLoan and other similar tribal lending institutions.

First Lending’s loan terms are comparable to payday or tribal installment lenders. They are marketed as “a great alternative to cash-out payday loans,” but they’re not much of an upgrade.

  • Customers with a principal amount of 0 to $5,500 ($1,500 limit for first-time purchasers) can receive loan funds.
  • An annual percentage rate (APR) of 600 to 795 percent.
  • Repayment terms with payment plans are offered weekly, bimonthly, and semi-monthly.

  • There are no prepayment penalties for paying off the loan obligations early.

FirstLoan presents an example of a typical loan accessible on its website, demonstrating how ludicrous these terms are. Only $0.17 of the first installment is applied to the principal. It is more than seven times the initial principal owing! The total loan cost to borrow is $3,387.82.
